Articulating TV Wall Mount Reinstallation: Fixing a DIY Disaster

Entertainment Pros March 14, 2026 4 min read

You finally bought that stunning new TV — the one with the vibrant colors and crystal-clear picture. You eagerly anticipate movie nights, immersive gaming, and just relaxing in front of your favorite shows. But then reality hits. You hired someone to mount it, or maybe you tackled the DIY route, and now your beautiful TV looks awful.

Crooked, wobbly, or just plain unsafe — the poor installation is detracting from the viewing experience and potentially damaging your wall. Sound familiar? Don't despair. Entertainment Pros has seen it all, and we're here to help. We specialize in articulating TV wall mount reinstallation and much more for your home entertainment needs.

A Wall Mount Gone Wrong in Pinellas County

Recently, we had the opportunity to rescue a TV installation project in Pinellas County that had gone sideways. To say it was a mess would be an understatement. As soon as we walked in, the issues were glaringly obvious.

Crooked Wall Plate Nightmare

Every single wall plate was noticeably crooked. This not only looks bad but also puts undue stress on the mount itself, increasing the risk of failure.

Loose and Unstable

Nothing was properly tightened. Bolts were loose, screws were stripped, and the entire assembly felt unstable. A TV — especially a larger one — mounted on an articulating arm needs to be rock solid. This installation was far from it.

Safety First

A TV hanging over your head should be as secure as humanly possible. This particular installation was a serious safety hazard. We cringed at the thought of what could have happened.

A Hole-y Mess

The sheer number of drill holes in the wall was astonishing. It looked like someone had used the wall as target practice. Each unnecessary hole weakened the wall and created more work to repair.

This situation highlights the importance of proper planning, the right tools, and a thorough understanding of wall mounting techniques. Getting it wrong can lead to cosmetic damage, structural issues, and — most importantly — a safety risk.

The Entertainment Pros Solution: Patching, Leveling, and Secure Mounting

Our approach to fixing this disaster was methodical and focused on restoring both the aesthetic appeal and structural integrity.

Patching and Repair

First, we carefully patched and repaired all the unnecessary drill holes, ensuring a smooth and even surface. This step is crucial for restoring the wall's appearance and providing a solid base for the new mount.

Precise Templating and Leveling

We used a template to precisely mark the new mounting points. Leveling is paramount — we use professional-grade levels to guarantee a perfectly aligned installation. A level TV makes all the difference in the viewing experience.

Secure Mounting Techniques

We used high-quality anchors and hardware appropriate for the wall type and the weight of the TV. We ensured that every bolt and screw was properly tightened to manufacturer specifications, providing maximum stability and peace of mind.

Expert Adjustments

Once the mount was securely installed, we carefully adjusted the articulating arm to achieve the optimal viewing angle. The TV is perfectly positioned for comfortable viewing from any seat in the room.

Why Doing It Right Takes Time

You might be wondering why a professional TV wall mount installation takes a few hours, while a rushed job can be completed in 30 minutes. The answer lies in the commitment to quality, safety, and attention to detail.

  • Thorough assessment of the wall type (drywall, plaster, concrete) and identifying any obstacles like studs, wiring, or plumbing.
  • Proper preparation — protecting the surrounding area, gathering the right tools and hardware, and carefully measuring and marking the mounting points.
  • Correct anchors and hardware chosen for the specific wall type and TV weight.
  • Attention to detail — leveling the mount, concealing cables, and adjusting the viewing angle.
  • Safety first — taking the necessary precautions so the TV is securely mounted and won't fall.
