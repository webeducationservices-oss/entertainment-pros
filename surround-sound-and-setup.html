Surround Sound and the Art of Setup: Where Tech Meets Configuration

February 5, 2026 | By Chip Tollefson | Entertainment Pros

Setting up surround sound is about far more than just plugging in speakers. The placement angle of each speaker, the distance from your listening position, and the acoustics of your room all play critical roles in how your system sounds. Get any of these wrong, and even the most expensive speakers will underperform.

In a standard 5.1 surround system, the front left and right speakers should be angled at roughly 22 to 30 degrees from center, while the surrounds sit at 110 to 120 degrees. The center channel goes directly in front of the listener, and the subwoofer placement depends on the room's dimensions and furnishings. For Dolby Atmos configurations with overhead channels, ceiling speaker placement becomes even more critical, requiring precise positioning relative to the primary seating area.

Surround sound isn't just about the speakers you buy — it's about where they go, how they're angled, and how the system is calibrated to your specific room.

After placement, calibration is where the real magic happens. We use professional-grade measurement tools to analyze the acoustic characteristics of your room and adjust each speaker's output level, delay timing, and equalization curve. This process ensures that sound arrives at your ears at the correct time from every direction, creating a truly immersive experience that makes you feel like you're inside the movie or concert. It's the difference between having surround sound and experiencing it.
